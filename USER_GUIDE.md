# Recall User Guide

## What is Recall?

Recall is your organization's memory system. It captures conversations, tracks decisions, and makes your team's knowledge searchable and accessible.

## Getting Started

### First Login
1. Use the invitation link sent by your admin
2. Create your password
3. Complete your profile (name, bio, timezone)
4. Start exploring

### Your Dashboard
The home page shows recent conversations across your organization. Use it to stay updated on what's happening.

## Core Features

### 1. Conversations

**Creating a Conversation**
- Click "New Conversation" button
- Choose type: Update, Question, Decision, or Proposal
- Write a clear title
- Add your content
- Use @mentions to notify specific people
- Use #tags to categorize topics

**Conversation Types**
- **Update**: Share progress, announcements, or status changes
- **Question**: Ask for help or clarification
- **Decision**: Document important choices
- **Proposal**: Suggest ideas or changes

**Replying**
- Click on any conversation to view details
- Write your reply at the bottom
- Reply to specific comments by clicking "Reply" on that comment
- Replies are threaded for easy following

### 2. Decisions

**Viewing Decisions**
- Go to Decisions page to see all organizational decisions
- Filter by status, impact level, or date
- View decision timeline to see how choices evolved

**Converting to Decision**
- Open any conversation
- Click "Convert to Decision"
- Select impact level (Low, Medium, High, Critical)
- Decision is now tracked separately

**Decision Details**
- Who made it
- When it was made
- Why it was made
- What alternatives were considered
- Current status

### 3. Knowledge Search

**Searching**
- Use the search bar in the header
- Or go to Knowledge page
- Type natural language queries
- Results show relevant conversations and decisions

**Ask Recall**
- Go to Ask Recall page
- Ask questions in plain English
- Get AI-powered answers with sources
- See confidence levels for each answer

### 4. Notifications

**Notification Types**
- Mentions: Someone tagged you with @yourname
- Replies: Someone replied to your conversation
- Decisions: Important decisions were made
- Organization Updates: Admin changed settings

**Managing Notifications**
- Click bell icon to see recent notifications
- Click "View All" for full notification page
- Mark as read or delete notifications
- Click "View" to go to the related conversation

### 5. Profile & Settings

**Your Profile**
- Upload profile picture
- Update name and bio
- Set timezone
- Change password
- View your activity stats

**Bookmarks**
- Bookmark important conversations
- Add private notes to bookmarks
- Access from your profile

## Tips for Success

### Writing Good Conversations
- **Clear titles**: Make it obvious what the conversation is about
- **Context first**: Explain why this matters
- **Be specific**: Include details, links, and examples
- **Use formatting**: Line breaks and structure help readability
- **Tag people**: Use @mentions to bring in the right people
- **Categorize**: Use #tags for topics

### Effective Searching
- Use natural language: "How do we handle customer refunds?"
- Be specific: "Decision about database migration 2024"
- Use keywords: Names, dates, project names
- Check recent decisions for quick answers

### Staying Organized
- Bookmark conversations you'll need later
- Add notes to bookmarks explaining why they're important
- Check notifications daily
- Use filters on Conversations and Decisions pages

## Mobile Usage

## Best Practices

### For Teams
1. **Document decisions immediately**: Don't wait, capture context while fresh
2. **Use consistent tags**: Agree on common #tags as a team
3. **Reply don't create**: Add to existing conversations instead of starting new ones
4. **Close conversations**: Mark resolved conversations as closed
5. **Review regularly**: Check Knowledge page weekly for insights

### For Individuals
1. **Set up notifications**: Stay informed without being overwhelmed
2. **Complete your profile**: Help others know who you are
3. **Use bookmarks**: Save important conversations for later
4. **Search first**: Check if your question was already answered
5. **Add context**: Explain your thinking, not just conclusions

## Common Questions

**Q: How do I delete a conversation?**
A: Only the author can delete. Open the conversation, click Edit, then Delete.

**Q: Can I edit my replies?**
A: Yes, click Edit on your own replies. Edit history is tracked.

**Q: Who can see my conversations?**
A: Everyone in your organization. Recall is designed for transparency.

**Q: How do I mute notifications?**
A: Go to your Profile → Preferences to customize notification settings.

**Q: What happens to deleted conversations?**
A: They're permanently removed. Consider closing instead of deleting.

**Q: Can I export conversations?**
A: Yes, admins can export decisions as ADR (Architecture Decision Records) markdown files.
